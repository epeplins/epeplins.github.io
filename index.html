Research

My research interests include the characterization of exoplanets and the broader applications of spectroscopy. In my current work, I'm investigating how different radiative transfer geometries impact the modeling of hot Jupiter emission spectra from 3D-GCMs. My mentors are Dr. Emily Rauscher and graduate student Thomas Kennedy.

Outreach & Teaching

My involvement in outreach is hugely important to me. My journey as a queer person in STEM drives my desire to engage others in science in positive, exciting ways. I have worked as a telescope operator for both introductory astronomy courses and public observatory open houses, and look forward to continuing this community engagement in the future.

Grad App Guide

Applying to graduate school is a draining enough process on its own, never mind understanding where (and when) to start! Several members of my undergraduate cohort and I have compiled a guide to aid in this process, including an advice document, example essays (both successful and not), and even a spreadsheet template for keeping track of your application information. If you have any questions (or you just need some advice), feel free to contact any of the emails listed in the 'README' document.
