Personal

More about me as a person inside and outside of science

Academics

I am a recent graduate of the University of Michigan (May 2024), where I earned a bachelor's in Astronomy & Astrophysics with Honors and am currently finishing up a project about radiative transfer geometry with Dr. Emily Rauscher. Beginning in Fall 2025, I will be pursuing my Ph.D. at the University of Maryland.

As a queer individual, I had a largely negative experience with STEM growing up, and I consequently spent the first two years of my undergraduate career as a film major. It was upon taking an introductory course in astronomy that I dicovered my affinity for this field, but more importantly it was the wonderful professors and grad students I met that gave me the courage to make the non-trivial switch. One of my driving goals since then has been to inspire that positive and encouraging enviroment in academia more widely.

Interests Beyond Planets

Outside of academia, I love making music! I've been a part of music ensembles all throughout my life, having played oboe for the past twelve or so years. I also play alto saxophone, and did so in the Michigan Marching Band as well as in a band with some of my other undergrad cohort. At the moment, I'm attempting to learn guitar.

Other hobbies of mine include writing, hiking, and drinking coffee. I've also been known to ride a horse on occasion.

I use they/them pronouns.
